## 📌 Overview

This repository documents a 3-day on-site operation supporting multiple physical activities under constrained conditions.

Rather than a single assigned role, this experience involved maintaining system continuity across multiple domains — including flow control, safety response, and resource management.

The focus was not task execution, but operating within a dynamic system where priorities continuously shifted.

---

## 📌 Problem

- Game flow was frequently interrupted across multiple sports (soccer, basketball, dodgeball)  
- Cold weather conditions reduced reaction speed and overall efficiency  
- Limited and inexperienced staff required flexible role adaptation  
- Injuries required immediate response without disrupting ongoing activities  
- Equipment and resources (balls, beverages, refrigerator) required continuous handling  
- Multiple activities ran in parallel, increasing coordination complexity  

---

## ⚙️ Approach

- Treated the environment as a **multi-layered operational system**  
- Focused on three core areas:
  - Flow continuity (games and activities)
  - Safety response (injury handling)
  - Resource management (equipment and cooling)  
- Switched roles dynamically based on real-time demand  
- Prioritized fast, minimal decision-making to reduce system friction  

---

## 🛠️ Implementation

### 1. Flow Maintenance (Games)
- Retrieved and returned balls immediately to prevent interruptions  
- Anticipated ball trajectories and positioned proactively  
- Managed out-of-bounds situations in basketball and dodgeball  
- Supported judgment calls when needed (secondary referee role)  

### 2. Safety Support (Medical Assistance)
- Responded to injured participants  
- Stabilized situations until further support arrived  
- Balanced safety handling with ongoing activity flow  

<img src="https://github.com/ulsidae/RSVP-systems/blob/main/%F0%9F%8C%8DPE-flow-support/img/20260428_161024.jpg" height="400" />

### 3. Resource & Equipment Management
- Organized and maintained event equipment  
- Transported and positioned a refrigerator under space constraints  
- Supported powered cooling to preserve ice and beverages  
- Ensured timely and consistent distribution of drinks  

### 4. Adaptive Role Switching
- Transitioned across roles (flow, logistics, safety) in real time  
- Prioritized actions based on urgency and system-level impact  

---

## 💡 Insights

- Real-world systems require **continuous role flexibility**, not fixed responsibilities  
- Flow, safety, and resources are tightly coupled — not independent domains  
- Small inefficiencies can escalate into system-wide disruption  
- Fast decisions under limited information are critical  
- Even unstructured environments can be managed through system-level thinking  
- Proactively understanding and confirming tasks is more effective than passively following instructions  
- Leadership pressure often reflects a focus on outcomes, not control  
- Observing from a junior role provided insight into effective leadership behavior
